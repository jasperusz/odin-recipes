Carbonara

Good men must eat good pasta.

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti
  • 150g (5 oz) guanciale or pancetta, diced (guanciale is traditional for its rich flavor)
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1-2 whole large eggs (for extra creaminess)
  • 100g (3.5 oz) Pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated (or Parmesan as a substitute)
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper, plus more for serving
  • Salt for pasta water (about 1 tbsp)
  • Optional: 1 clove garlic, whole (for flavoring the pan, then removed)

Preparation

  1. Prepare the Pasta:Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add spaghetti and cook according to package instructions until al dente (usually 8-10 minutes). Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. Cook the Guanciale:While pasta cooks, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add diced guanciale (no oil needed—it renders its own fat). Cook until crispy and golden, about 5-7 minutes. If using, add a whole garlic clove for flavor and remove it once browned. Remove from heat if pasta isn't ready yet to avoid overcooking.
  3. Make the Sauce:In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks, whole egg(s), grated cheese, and black pepper until smooth. This creates the base for the creamy sauce.
  4. Combine Everything:Add drained spaghetti to the skillet with guanciale over low heat. Pour in the egg mixture and toss quickly to coat the pasta. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time (start with 1/2 cup) to create a silky sauce—stir vigorously to emulsify without scrambling the eggs. The heat from the pasta and pan should cook the eggs gently. Season with more pepper if desired.
  5. Serve immediately with extra grated cheese and black pepper on top. For authenticity, avoid cream, onions, or peas—these are common but non-traditional additions. Pair with a crisp white wine for a complete meal.
